### Proposed Edge-Case Tests

**Test 1: Empty Tile Ingestion**  
- **Scenario:** Room receives a tile with empty content (`{}` or `null`).  
- **Expected:** Room should log a P0 violation (negative space) and reject the tile without crashing.  
- **Rationale:** Ensures robustness against malformed fleet traffic.

**Test 2: P0 Violation Logging**  
- **Scenario:** Agent submits a tile that directly violates a known P0 rule (e.g., attempts to write to protected memory).  
- **Expected:** Room should capture the violation, increment a metric, and store a ghost tile for training.  
- **Rationale:** Validates that DeadbandRoom enforces doctrine and learns from errors.

**Test 3: Tile Queue Overflow**  
- **Scenario:** Simulate rapid tile submission exceeding the room's queue capacity (e.g., 10,000 tiles/sec).  
- **Expected:** Room should throttle or shed load, not crash, and preserve P1 channels (e.g., log overflow, discard oldest).  
- **Rationale:** Tests resilience under fleet-scale load.

**Test 4: Concurrent Tile Processing**  
- **Scenario:** Multiple agents submit tiles simultaneously from different threads/processes.  
- **Expected:** Room should process tiles without race conditions, deadlocks, or state corruption.  
- **Rationale:** Ensures thread safety for multi-agent fleets.

**Test 5: Ensign Export During Training**  
- **Scenario:** Request a LoRA adapter export while the room is actively training on incoming tiles.  
- **Expected:** Room should either queue the request, snapshot state, or reject with a busy signal—but not corrupt training.  
- **Rationale:** Validates integration with plato-ensign under realistic conditions.
